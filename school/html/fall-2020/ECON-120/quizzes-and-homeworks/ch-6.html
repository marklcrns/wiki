Alternative Unemployment Measures. An economy has 120,000,000 people employed, 7, 000, 000 unemployed, and 5, 000, 000 discouraged workers. The conventional measure of the unemployment rate is 5.51 percent.

The best alternative measure that takes into account discouraged workers is an unemployment rate of 9.09 percent.

\text{ Conventional Unemployment Rate } = \frac{\text{ Unemployed }}{\text{ Labor Force + Unemployed }} \times 100

\text{ Best Alternative Unemployment Rate } = \frac{\text{ Unemployed + Discouraged workers }}{\text{ Labor Force + Discouraged workers }} \times 100
