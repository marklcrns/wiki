Section 1.11: The Scales of Notation

  1. If m and n are positive integers and n > 1, then m can be represented in terms of n in one and in only one way in the form m = a_{0}n^{h} + a_{1}n^{h - 1} + \cdots + a_{h - 1}n + a_{h},

    where

    a_{0} \neq 0, 0 \le a_{i} < n, \;\;\; i = 0,1,2,\cdots, h.

  2. Any positive integer can be represented in one and in only one way as a sum of different powers of 2.
