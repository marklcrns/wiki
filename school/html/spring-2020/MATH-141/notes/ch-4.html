Chapter 4: Angles Triangle and Areas

4.1 What is Trig

  • Handout Trig Notes 1

Degrees

  • Degrees are units of measurement for angles. One degree is defined to be the measurement of the angle created by slicing up a circle into 360 equal slices.

Radians

  • Radians are units of measurement for angles. One Radian is defined to be the measurement of the angle created by the distance of one radius along part of the circumference of a circle.

Definition of \(\pi\)

  • the exact number of radians that fit in a half-circle.
  • \(\pi \approx 3.14\)

Converting degrees to radians: Multiply degrees by \(\frac{\pi rad}{180 \deg}\)

Converting radians to degrees: Multiply radians by \(\frac{180 \deg}{\pi rad}\)

Example: Convert \(75 \deg\) to radian

\[ \begin{aligned} 75 \deg &= 75 deg \cdot \frac{\pi rad}{180 \deg} \\ &= 75 \cdot \frac{\pi rad}{180} \\ &\approx 1.309 rad \end{aligned} \]

Complementary angles

  • Two angles that adds to \(90^{\circ}.\)

Supplementary angle

  • Two angles that adds to \(180^{\circ}.\)

4.2 Angles and Circles

  • Handout Trig Notes 2

Video Lecture 1

  • VIDEO 1: Trigonometry Introduction to Circles Areas of and Circumference

Video Lecture 1 Notes

Formulas

  • Area of a quadrilaterals (rectangle, square, parallelogram, trapezoid)
    • \(A = b \cdot h\), where \(b\) as the base and \(h\) as the height.
  • Area of a circle (6:12)
    • \(A = \pi r^{2}\), where \(r\) as the radius.
  • Circumference of a circle
    • \(C = 2 \pi r\)
  • Area of a sector (14:41)
    • \(A_{s} = \pi r^{2} \cdot \frac{x}{2\pi}\)
      • \(A_{s} = \frac{x}{2} r^{2}\), where \(x\) in radians.
    • \(A_{s} = \pi r^{2} \cdot \frac{x^{\circ}}{360^{\circ}}\), where \(x\) in degrees.
  • Arc-length (22:57)
    • \(S = \frac{x}{2\pi} \cdot 2\pi r\)
      • \(S = x \cdot r\), where \(x\) in radians.
    • \(S = \frac{x^{\circ}}{360^{\circ}} \cdot 2 \pi r\), where \(x\) in degrees.

Sector

  • Sectors are portions of a circle

4.3 Volumes and Pythagoras

  • Handout Trig Notes 3
  • Handout Trig Notes 4

Video Lecture 1

  • VIDEO 1: Introduction to volumes, cones, cylinders spheres

Video Lecture 1 Notes

Formulas

  • Volume of a cylinder (3:10)
    • \(V = A \cdot h\), where \(A\) is the area of the bottom and \(h\) as the upright height.
    • Slanted cylinders or any similar shapes does not change the volume as it is upright, as long as the upright height remains the same.
  • Surface Area of a cylinder (24:46)
    • \(SA = 2\pi \cdot r \cdot h\)
  • Volume of a prism or Volume of a cone (7:37)
    • \(V = \frac{1}{3}h \cdot A\)
  • Surface Area of a cone
    • \(\frac{1}{3}\pi r^{2} \cdot h\)
  • Volume of a sphere (27:45)
    • \(V = \frac{4}{3}\pi \cdot r^{3}\)
  • Surface Area of a sphere
    • \(4 \cdot \pi r^{2}\)

Daaabz Questions

  • Surface Area of truncated cylindrical cone
    • \(\frac{r + R}{2} \cdot 2\pi \cdot s\), where \(r\) is the radius of the top circle, \(R\) is the radius of the base circle, and \(s\) as the distance from outline of the base to the outline of the top circle.

4.5 Triangles 30-60 and 45-45

Video Lecture 1

  • VIDEO 1: ratios for 30-60 triangles are easy

Video Lecture 1 Notes

  • If ratios are known, one side is needed.
  • Small angles produces small sides, big angles produces big sides.
  • Across \(30^{\circ}\) is the smallest side.
  • Across \(60^{\circ}\) is the biggest side.
  • Across \(90^{\circ}\) is the hypotenuse side.
  • 30-60 Theorem (6:00)
    • From the small side to hypotenuse, \(s \cdot 2 = h\)
    • From the small side to big side, \(s \cdot \sqrt{3} = S\)
    • From hypotenuse to small side, \(\frac{h}{2} = s\)
    • From big side to small side, \(\frac{S}{\sqrt{3}} = s\)
  • 30-60 Theorem proof (21:46)
